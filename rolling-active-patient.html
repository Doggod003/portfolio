Rolling Active Patient Reporting

The challenge: A correctional healthcare operations team needed to quickly answer "who is currently active on this treatment table?" without manually filtering data or waiting for analyst reports. Existing reporting required manual updates and was prone to errors.

The solution

Built a self-serve Power BI dashboard that dynamically calculates active patient status based on rolling treatment-date windows. Instead of static snapshots, the dashboard uses DAX measures to determine who's "active" relative to a configurable lookback period—patients move in and out automatically as their treatment dates shift.

What I built

  • Custom DAX measures for rolling-window active status
  • Drill-down from cohort counts to patient-level detail
  • Dynamic filters for date ranges and treatment tables
  • Automated refresh synced to source data

Technical stack

  • Power BI DAX
  • SQL (source extraction)
  • DateTable relationships
  • Real-time refresh scheduling

Impact

  • Eliminated recurring manual reports
  • Answers provided in seconds, not hours
  • Reusable DAX pattern across other tables
  • Self-serve means no analyst bottleneck

Why this matters

This dashboard demonstrates the core principle of my work: taking a messy operational question and turning it into a clean, data-driven answer. Operations teams shouldn't need to wrangle spreadsheets or wait for reports—they should be able to ask "who's active right now?" and get an instant, accurate answer. The DAX pattern I built is reusable across other treatment tables and healthcare cohort scenarios.
