HOA Subdivision Outreach System

GIS-driven targeted outreach for landscaping and local services

The problem: Landscaping clients wanted to target specific high-value neighborhoods, not blanket zip codes. They needed a way to identify HOA-governed subdivisions, visualize boundaries, and pull mailing lists for homeowner-occupied parcels. The underlying data lived in Dauphin County GIS exports—technical, fragmented, and not designed for marketing use.

The solution

Built a GIS-driven targeting system that translates public county parcel data into a marketing tool. Pulled subdivision and parcel boundaries from Dauphin County GIS, mapped HOA-governed neighborhoods, and assembled an Excel workbook that ties subdivision-level targeting to mailing-list-ready outputs. Non-technical landscaping clients could now mail and door-knock specific subdivisions instead of guessing at zip codes.

Core insight: The data already exists in county GIS systems. The barrier isn't data availability—it's converting raw GIS exports into something a small landscaping business can actually use to find customers.

Why this matters

This project demonstrates something often overlooked: the ability to take freely available public data (county GIS), structure it, and convert it into a tool that directly helps a client win business. It's not flashy, but it's valuable.

This project highlights:

  • Public data literacy — Knowing where county GIS exports live, how to interpret them, and how to repurpose them for non-GIS use
  • Cross-functional execution — Pulling data, mapping boundaries, structuring outputs, and translating into something a non-technical landscaper can use
  • Practical business thinking — Understanding that small services businesses need targeted outreach tools, not generic marketing advice
  • Scalability mindset — Building the first deployment in a way that's extensible to other townships, counties, and verticals without starting from scratch

Extensibility

The same approach is applicable to other Dauphin County townships, neighboring counties, and other service verticals (real estate prospecting, contractor outreach, financial services). The core pattern—public GIS data → structured targeting tool—repeats.
